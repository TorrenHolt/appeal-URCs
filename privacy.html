Site Policy

Privacy Policy

What we collect, how we use it, who can access it, and your rights — including how to ask us to delete your information.

Plain-language summary: We collect only what you voluntarily submit through the sign-up form (email, name, approximate case date, county). We do not sell it, share it with DCYF or any government agency, or use it for advertising. We store it in a third-party form service. It could theoretically be subpoenaed in legal proceedings. You can ask us to delete your submission at any time.

1 — Who operates this site

This site is maintained by Torrence Becker, the plaintiff in Becker v. DCYF et al., Civil No. 1:24-cv-00430-JL-TSM, pending in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Hampshire. It is not affiliated with DCYF, DHHS, or any government agency. References to "we," "us," or "our" throughout this policy refer to the site operator.

2 — What we collect

When you submit the sign-up form, we may receive:

  • Email address — required to send you updates
  • Name — optional
  • Approximate year of URC finding — optional; used only to understand how many families were affected and in what period
  • County — optional; used only to understand geographic scope

We do not collect Social Security numbers, case file numbers, the names of children, details about any specific DCYF investigation, or any other sensitive personal information. Please do not include that information in any submission.

We also collect standard server logs (IP address, browser type, referring page) through our hosting provider (GitHub Pages). These logs are governed by GitHub's own privacy policy and are not used by us for any purpose.

3 — How we use your information

We use submitted information solely to:

  • Send you updates about this litigation and related administrative proceedings as they develop
  • Understand, in aggregate, how many NH families were affected by URC findings and in what time period

We do not use your information for advertising, marketing, fundraising, or any commercial purpose. We do not sell, rent, or trade your information to any third party.

4 — Who can access your information

Access to submitted information is limited to the site operator and, where necessary, any attorneys or legal advocates directly assisting with Becker v. DCYF et al. or the related AAU administrative appeals. Your information will not be shared with DCYF, DHHS, the NH Attorney General's Office, or any government agency.

5 — Legal process and subpoena risk

Important This site is connected to active federal civil rights litigation. In any litigation, parties may seek discovery of documents and records held by others. There is a possibility — which we cannot rule out — that a court could order production of information you submitted to this site in response to a subpoena or other legal process. We would comply with any valid court order, as required by law.

We have no reason to believe this will occur, and we have no current intention of using sign-up information in any court proceeding. However, you should be aware that no digital record is completely beyond the reach of legal process. If this risk concerns you, do not submit your information — you can still follow this site and reach out by email through a separate account of your choosing.

We will notify you by email if we receive legal process requiring disclosure of your information, to the extent we are legally permitted to do so.

6 — Data storage and security

Sign-up submissions are processed and stored through a third-party form service. That service's own privacy policy governs how it handles data on its infrastructure. We take reasonable steps to limit who has access to collected information, but no digital storage is guaranteed to be secure against all possible breaches.

We do not store payment information because we do not collect any.

7 — How long we keep your information

We retain submitted information for the duration of the litigation and related administrative proceedings, unless you ask us to delete it sooner. When the proceedings conclude and the record is closed, we will delete all personally identifiable information that is no longer needed.

8 — Your right to delete your submission

You can ask us to delete your submission at any time, for any reason, with no explanation required.

To request deletion, email us at nhfamiliesrights@gmail.com with the subject line "Delete my information" and include the email address you used when signing up. We will confirm deletion within five business days.

Deletion removes your information from our records. It does not affect any information already shared with attorneys or advocates in connection with the litigation, or any aggregate statistics we have already compiled (which do not identify individuals).

9 — Children

This site is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If a parent or guardian believes a child has submitted information, please contact us for immediate deletion.

10 — Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the effective date below and, where feasible, notify you by email if you have signed up for updates. Continued use of this site after a policy change constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Questions

Questions about this policy or your information can be directed to nhfamiliesrights@gmail.com.
